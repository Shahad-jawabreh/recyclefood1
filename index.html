How we do it

Recycling food waste is an important step in reducing environmental impact and promoting sustainability. Here are three steps to recycle food waste

  • - Separate food waste from regular trash.
  • - Store food waste in a designated container.
  • - Educate household members about proper food waste separation.

Collection

  • - Set up a compost pile or bin.
  • - Add compostable food scraps like fruits, vegetables, coffee grounds
  • - Maintain the compost by turning it regularly and keeping it moist.

Composting

  • - Check if your community offers curbside pickup of food waste.
  • - Look for local community composting facilities or drop-off locations if curbside pickup is not available.
  • - Store food waste in a designated container.

Fertilizers Vs Manure

Organic Fertilizer

  • source Derived from natural materials such as compost, manure, and plant-based materials.
  • Nutrients are released slowly and gradually as organic matter breaks down
  • Improves soil structure and fertility,enhancing microbial activity and long-term soil health.

Chemical Fertilizer

  • Manufactured from synthetic chemicals, including ammonia, urea, and potassium,salts.
  • Immediate Impact Since chemical fertilizers release nutrients quickly, they can have an almost immediate impact on plant growth.
  • May lead to soil degradation over time,as it may disrupt the soil's natural , balance and reduce microbial activity.

Is this process environmentally friendly?

The environmental friendliness of food recycling processes can vary. On the positive side, food recycling reduces food waste, generates energy from waste, and recycles valuable nutrients. However, some processes can be energy and resource-intensive. The overall impact depends on the specific methods and local factors.
